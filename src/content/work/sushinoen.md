Sushinoen is a Japanese restaurant tucked away in Whitechapel, and the brief was to give their online presence the same care that goes into the food. The site had originally been built on Squarespace, and while it worked well enough as a starting point, it became clear fairly early on that it wasn't the right long-term home for the project.

The decision to migrate to Wix came out of a conversation with the client about what happened after launch. Squarespace offered a polished editing experience, but the day-to-day updates (swapping menu items, refreshing photography, adjusting opening hours) were friction points the owners didn't want to rely on me for every time. Wix gave them a more intuitive editor and a content model they felt confident maintaining themselves once I stepped away, and that ended up being the single most important constraint shaping the build.

I worked alongside a designer to translate their vision into something that felt warm, considered, and distinctly Sushinoen rather than another templated restaurant site. A lot of the work happened inside Wix itself (custom CSS, embedded code, small bits of JavaScript) to smooth over the rough edges of the platform and bring the design closer to what we'd originally sketched out. Typography, spacing, and the way imagery breathed on the page all needed nudging beyond what the editor offered out of the box.

The handover was the part I was most proud of. The client walked away with a site that looked the way we wanted it to look, but more importantly, one they could actually run. No tickets, no dependency on a developer for a menu change, just a platform that matched the way they worked.
